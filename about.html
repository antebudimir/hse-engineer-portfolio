Practical solution oriented HSE Engineer

Some of the sentences from my childhood that still ring in my memory are: "Watch out for this and watch out for that; don't walk there, it's dangerous; don't go near the edge, you'll fall. At some point I got the impression that all that life comes down to is being careful. And yet I have seen my parents so many times disregarding those same safety instructions that they were giving to me like they are not endangered in the same way I was.

So what changes when we grow up? How come we start disregarding guidelines that protected us during our first years of life? We're creatures of habits and when we repeat certain actions enough times that they become a second nature to us, (over)confidence kicks in. These are just some thoughts that I like to ponder about and discuss with my colleagues and with people that attend my lectures.
